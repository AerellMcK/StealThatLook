Ornithorhynchus anatinus

Platypus
By Aerell McKnight
Commonly found near bodies of freshwater such as creeks, slow rivers, lakes and rivers, and built water storages such as farm dams.

Their niches are typically in their burrows
Streamlined body with a bill and broad flat tail

Short limbs with webbed feet

Dense dark brown to reddish brown fur with light brown/silver underfur (Sometimes teal)
  • Webbed limbs for swimming
  • Water-proof fur
  • Strong claws for burrowing
  • Venomous
